History Of Android Mascot

You probably know that the android operating system, maintained by google, is one of the most used for mobile devices worldwide. But, you may not know that your friendly mascot has a very curious name and history! So follow this article to learn a lot about this little robot.

First Version

The first try to create a mascot surged in 2007 and came of a developer called Dan Morrill. He says that opened the linkscape (free software to image vetorizing) and created your own version of a robot. The objective was just personify the sistem to his team, there was no request from his business about the creation of a mascot.

This bizarre first version was called in pleasure to creator. Was the Dandroids.

A New Mascot Appears

The idea of having a mascot was maturing and the mission was passed on to a professional in the area. Russian illustrator Irina Blok, also a Google employee, was tasked with representing the little robot in a more pleasant way.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything graphically with few strokes and more flat. The drawing should also generate quick identification with those who look at it. Then came Bugdroid, the new Android mascot.

The main inspiration for the features of the new Bugdroid came from those little dolls that illustrate bathroom doors to indicate the gender of each door. Legend has it that the artist was creating on her desk in the Google office and looked at the side of the bathrooms and the identification was immediate: simple, clean, objective.

Want To Learn More?

Another curious subject regarding Android is that each version has always been named after a sweet, alphabetical order from version 1.5 to 9.0.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 2.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lolipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, Android Q did not exist, because Google decided to put an end to this fun practice and began to use numbers, which gave rise to Android 10.
